About Hazrat El Sheikh Syed Mubarik Ali Shah Gillani (ra)

Esteemed Lineage & Heritage. El‑Sheikh Syed Mubarik Ali Shah Gillani Hashimi – “Al‑Hashmi wal‑Hassani wa‑Hussaini Qadiree” – traces his noble ancestry to the family of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) through both Imam Hassan and Imam Hussein, and heads the Qadiri Sufi silsila in his time. His family served as traditional custodians of the Mian Mir shrine in Lahore, reflecting a multi‑generational legacy of service to Tasawwuf.

Formative Years & Education. His grandfather, Syed Inayat Ali Shah Gillani (ra), foretold his blessed birth and mission “to reignite the lamp of the Qadri silsila.” He later graduated from the University of the Punjab, where he founded the Adventure Club for climbers and mountaineers.

Mission in America. In 1980 he founded The Muslims of America (TMOA) as an answered prayer of American Muslims seeking hedaya (guidance). His community‑building extended to residential communities such as Holy Islamberg (NY) and Holy Islamville (SC), interfaith bridge‑building, humanitarian service, and a network of organizations devoted to education, youth, women’s leadership, veterans, and relief.

Educational Leadership. He established the International Qur’anic Open University (IQOU) to center Qur’anic learning. Under his successor, Sheikha Syeda Zainab Gillani, IQOU Theological College partnered with Mali’s Mamma Haidara Commemorative Library to bring ancient Islamic manuscripts to the U.S., strengthening global scholarship links.
